Understanding Water Purity Grades

Laboratory water is classified primarily by its physical and chemical properties. The American Society for Testing and Materials (ASTM) provides the most widely recognized standard: ASTM D1193.

Type I: Ultrapure Water

The gold standard for analytical chemistry. With a resistivity of 18.2 MΩ·cm, it is required for HPLC, GC, Mass Spec, and cell culture.

Type II: Analytical Grade

The "workhorse" of the lab. Ideal for reagent preparation, buffer making, and clinical analyzers. Resistivity > 1.0 MΩ·cm.

Type III/IV: General Purpose

Used for glassware rinsing, heating baths, and as feedwater for Type I systems.

Choosing Based on Instrument Requirements

Using the wrong grade of water can lead to baseline noise, ghost peaks in chromatography, and expensive damage to sensitive internal components.

  • HPLC/UHPLC: Requires Type I (low TOC) to prevent column fouling.
  • ICP-MS: Requires Type I (ultra-low ions) to avoid background interference.
  • Microbiology: Requires Type I or II (sterile/endotoxin-free) for culture media.
  • Autoclaves: Requires Type II or III to prevent scale buildup.
